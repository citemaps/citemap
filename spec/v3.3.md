## Overview

v3.3 is the **consolidation release**. Where v3.1 and v3.2 each added a single focused feature on top of the prior version, v3.3 packages a deliberate batch of structural additions, field-set extensions, and taxonomy cleanups that have accumulated since v3.2 shipped. The motivation: cutting one well-planned v3.3 is cheaper for the ecosystem (Yoast, Rank Math, next-seo, in-the-wild parsers, the publicly available CiteMap validator) than spreading the same set of changes across three accidental point releases.

The v3.3 cut delivers five categories of change, in order of architectural significance:

1. **JSON-LD `@graph` extension.** v3.3 makes citemap.json an explicit JSON-LD document, adding optional top-level `@graph[]` and `relationships[]` arrays for declaring multi-entity networks ã franchises, parent/subsidiary chains, multi-location practices, practitioner networks, multi-vertical companies, successor/predecessor entities. The current spec describes a single entity well; v3.3 adds the vocabulary to describe how that entity relates to others. AI engines that already parse schema.org `@graph` get richer entity disambiguation; tools that don't read the extension see a v3.2-shaped document with a few additional `brand.*` fields.

2. **Foundational sections (always-on contract).** v3.3 separates "verticals" (user-selected categorical classification, optional, multi-able) from "foundational sections" (always emitted regardless of vertical selection). The v3.0 flat "modules" catalog conflated the two. v3.3 documents the contract: every citemap.json carries `brand`, `citationContract`, `temporalRecord`, `trust.perField`, `answerContent`, `brand.team`, `brand.products`, `brand.sameAs`, `policy` (new), and `verification` (promoted from optional module to foundational). Producers can no longer suppress these; consumers can rely on them being present.

3. **Constituent field additions (14 fields).** Fields that have been emitted by Studio's reference implementation ahead of the spec ã `brand.legalName`, `brand.email`, `brand.taxonomy` (revisions), `brand.team[]` (cross-vertical core), `brand.teamPageUrl`, `brand.products[]`, `brand.locations[]`, `brand.subBrands[]`, `brand.productCategories[]` + `brand.serviceCategories[]`, `brand.services[]`, `brand.storefronts[]`, `brand.publications[]`, plus the per-vertical field promotions that surfaced during the 2026-Q2 multi-vertical dogfood. v3.3 documents the canonical shape, semantics, and derivation rules for each. v3.2 emitters continue to validate; v3.3 producers and consumers gain a richer agreed vocabulary.

4. **Modules ã verticals + foundational reframe.** The 15 user-pickable verticals (with renames: `events` ã `events-venue`, `health` ã `healthcare`, `legal` ã `legal-services`, `finance` ã `financial-services`) replace the v3.0 module catalog as the user-facing classification layer. Legacy module keys are emitted alongside the new vertical keys for one release window so v3.0/v3.1/v3.2 consumers reading hardcoded keys continue to find them. Three v3.0 modules are dropped (`business-IP` consolidated into `brand`; `people` superseded by `brand.team`; `places` superseded by `brand.location` + per-location entries in `brand.locations[]`).

5. **Vertical taxonomy retirements.** Four entries that were structurally axis-pretending-to-be-vertical retire from the recommended `taxonomy.primaryVertical` registry: `local-business`, `manufacturer`, `membership-organization`, and `industrial-services-b2b`. Each stays as a valid derived `brand.siteType` value for back-compat (the siteType enum is unchanged), but stops being a recommended `primaryVertical` option. Producers with these as their primary vertical get a migration path to the closest real vertical with a low-confidence flag. Multi-vertical equality stays intact ã no primary-vs-secondary split.

### Design Principle: additive, never breaking

Every change in v3.3 is purely additive at the structural level. A v3.2 citemap.json passes v3.3 validation with at most a one-line `version` bump. v3.3-specific fields (`@graph`, `relationships`, the constituent additions, the foundational sections) are either optional or always-emitted-by-the-emitter but optional-on-the-consumer-side. Renamed module keys are emitted alongside their legacy counterparts; taxonomy retirements affect the registry's recommended-values list but not the validator's accepted-values list.

The cost of the consolidation is that v3.3 documents are slightly fatter than v3.2 documents (the always-on foundational sections + the dual-write renamed keys), and producers eventually have to drop the legacy keys at v3.4. The benefit is that downstream tooling ã schema generators, AI engine training pipelines, the citemaps.org validator, third-party parsers ã gets one coordinated upgrade moment instead of three.

### Design Principle: JSON-LD native, not graph-format-native

v3.3 adopts schema.org's `@graph` mechanism for multi-entity documents rather than introducing a generic graph format (JSON Graph Format, GraphSON, etc.). The rationale is adoption-shaped: JSON-LD `@graph` is what every AI engine training pipeline already parses, what every SEO tool that emits structured data already understands, and what every schema.org-compliant Knowledge Graph indexes from. Picking a graph format the engines don't natively read defeats citemap.json's purpose. The architectural decision and the counterarguments are documented at length in [ôÏ1.1](#11-why-json-ld-graph-and-not-json-graph-format).

### Design Principle: foundational always-on as a differentiation moat

The decision to make 10 sections always-on (rather than user-toggleable per the v3.0 module model) is partly architectural cleanup and partly strategic. AI engines reading a citemap.json get a guaranteed minimum of structured signal: identity (`brand`), provenance (`citationContract`), temporal record (`temporalRecord`), trust (`trust.perField`), authored answers (`answerContent`), team (`brand.team`), products (`brand.products`), social presence (`brand.sameAs`), publisher trust (`policy`), and verification claims (`verification`). Competitors emitting bare schema.org Organization look thin next to a citemap that carries 10 foundational sections per regen. The "always-on foundational" framing is concrete and pitchable in marketing copy and as differentiated structural signal AI engines weight.
